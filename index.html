Our Mission

One Weekend
a Month.
Two Missions.

The New Jersey Army National Guard serves a dual mission: providing trained, ready units to support the U.S. Army overseas — and protecting life and property here at home during natural disasters and civil emergencies.

As citizen-soldiers, our Guardsmen maintain civilian careers and lives while remaining ready to answer the call whenever and wherever needed — neighbors, colleagues, and protectors of the Garden State.

Here's exactly what the journey from enlistment to your first drill weekend looks like.

Week 0

Enlistment & MEPS

Meet with a recruiter, complete paperwork, and report to the Military Entrance Processing Station (MEPS) for a full medical exam, ASVAB, and oath of enlistment.

Weeks 1–10

Basic Combat Training (BCT)

Ten intensive weeks at Fort Jackson, SC or Fort Leonard Wood, MO. You'll develop combat skills, physical fitness, first aid, and land navigation. Graduation is a ceremony your family can attend.

Specialized school for your MOS. Length ranges from 4 weeks to 52 weeks. You'll earn hands-on skills, certifications, and credentials that transfer directly to civilian careers.

Service Options

Which Branch
is Right for You?

Understand the key differences between the NJ Guard, Army Reserve, and Active Duty before you commit.

🛡️
NJ Army National Guard
Part-Time · Dual Mission
  • 1 weekend/month + 2 weeks/year
  • Keep civilian job & career
  • 100% NJ tuition waiver
  • Up to $20K enlistment bonus
  • State & federal deployment
  • Local armory — stay near home
  • Pension at age 60 (after 20 yr)
  • Deployment risk: moderate
  • No full-time BAH (except activation)
⚙️
Army Reserve
Part-Time · Federal Only
  • 1 weekend/month + 2 weeks/year
  • Keep civilian job & career
  • GI Bill benefits (lower rate)
  • Enlistment bonuses available
  • Federal deployments only
  • Units may be farther from home
  • Pension at age 60 (after 20 yr)
  • Deployment risk: moderate
  • No NJ state tuition program
🎖️
Active Duty Army
Full-Time · Regular Army
  • Full-time commitment required
  • Civilian career paused
  • Full GI Bill (36 months)
  • Higher base pay
  • Full housing allowance (BAH)
  • Assigned to any post worldwide
  • Pension after 20 yr (any age)
  • Deployment risk: high
  • Full TRICARE coverage
Physical Readiness

ACFT — Army Combat
Fitness Test

The ACFT is the Army's physical fitness standard. You don't need to pass it before enlisting — your recruiter will help you prepare. Here's what to train toward:

🏋️

3-Rep Max Deadlift

140–340 lbs

Tests lower-body and grip strength. Practice squats, Romanian deadlifts, and leg press.

🏋️‍♂️

Standing Power Throw

4.5–12.5 meters

Tests explosive power with a 10 lb medicine ball throw. Work on hip drive and core.

💪

Hand-Release Push-Ups

10–60 reps (2 min)

Tests muscular endurance. Focus on full range of motion and core engagement.

🏃

Sprint-Drag-Carry

1:33–3:00 (50m × 5)

Tests anaerobic endurance across five 50m lanes — sprint, sled drag, lateral, carry, sprint.

🤸

Plank

2:09–3:40 hold

Tests core stability. Replaced sit-ups in the new standard. Keep a flat back and neutral spine.

🏃‍♂️

Two-Mile Run

13:30–21:00

Tests aerobic endurance. Build a base with 3–4 runs per week and gradually increase distance.

💡

Don't worry if you can't meet all standards today. Recruiters provide fitness guidance and you'll have time to train before your report date.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked
Questions

The standard commitment is one weekend per month (Friday evening through Sunday) and two weeks of Annual Training each summer — roughly 39 days per year. Additional time may be required for schools, special training, or state activation during emergencies.

Yes — that's the entire point. USERRA legally protects your civilian job during Guard service. Most Guardsmen simultaneously work full-time and attend college. The NJNG 100% tuition waiver is specifically for members enrolled in NJ state schools.

The minimum AFQT score to enlist in the Army National Guard is 31 with a high school diploma, and 50 with a GED. Specific MOS positions have additional line-score requirements. A higher score means more career options — free ASVAB prep resources are available through your recruiter.

The Guard can and does deploy, but frequency is much lower than Active Duty. NJNG units have deployed to Iraq, Afghanistan, and peacekeeping missions. Most Guardsmen deploy 0–1 times during their career. You'll receive advance notice, and USERRA protects your job throughout.

Common disqualifiers include felony convictions, certain medical conditions, and failure to pass the ASVAB or physical. However, many conditions can be waived. Age, minor criminal history, education, and some medical issues all have waiver processes. Talk to a recruiter honestly — they're here to help, not to disqualify you.

Standard enlistment contracts are 8 years total service obligation (TSO), typically split as 3–6 years active (drilling) with the remainder in the Individual Ready Reserve (IRR). Re-enlistment bonuses are available after your initial contract.

The general age range is 17–42 years old (parental consent required at 17). Prior service members may be eligible up to age 59, depending on remaining service years and break in service. Use our Eligibility Checker above or contact a recruiter for your specific situation.

Guard members have access to TRICARE Reserve Select, a low-cost premium-based health plan. During activation (30+ days of federal or state orders), you receive full TRICARE coverage at no cost. TRICARE Dental and Vision programs are also available year-round.

The Split Training Option lets juniors enlist and attend Basic Combat Training (BCT) the summer between junior and senior year, then complete AIT the following summer after graduation. You earn the enlistment bonus and Guard benefits while finishing high school on time.
